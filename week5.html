Sprint Retrospective

Week 5 Sprint Retrospective

What went well?

This week, I successfully transitioned from lo-fi prototypes to developing the brand identity for my app. I created a stylescape, finalised the colour palette, and designed brand illustrations that matched the youthful, quirky, and approachable tone of voice. I also made strong progress on the design system, working toward high-fidelity prototypes. These developments kept me aligned with building a visually appealing brand direction alongside refining the app's structure.

What didn't go well?

I sometimes got caught up refining small visual details, which slowed down my overall progress, and I learnt that not to think too much of one thing and just let it flow.

What can be improved?

I learned that while attention to visual details is important, it's important to set clear time limits for each task to avoid perfectionism which is slowing my speed down. Prioritising core functionality first, then polishing later, would be a more efficient workflow.

What actions will I take for the next sprint?

  • Focus on completing hi-fi prototypes with key user flows fully functional
  • Prepare for hi-fi usability testing by drafting a testing plan
  • Keep visual refinement secondary to functionality and flow clarity
  • Continue maintaining brand consistency across the app UI
